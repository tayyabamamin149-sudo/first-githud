EXPERIMENT # 01

OBJECT:

To verify Newton's law of cooling.

Drawing graph between time and temperature. Calculation of cooling constant.

APPARATUS

Calorimeter, Thermometer, Stop watch, Iron stand, Beaker, Hot water.

TASKS

  1. Set up the apparatus.
  2. Note fall in temperature after every minute.
  3. Calculate cooling constant after every 5 minute.
  4. Draw graph between temperature and time.

Working Formula:

  • K = Fall in temp. in the interval
  • Mean temp. in interval-Room temp

    • Where "K" is Newton's cooling constant and define as "The rate of cooling proportional to the difference of temperature between the hot body and its surrounding provide the temperature difference is small".

    PROCEDURE

    1. Set up the apparatus as in figure and heat water to about 80'C
    2. Fill the calorimeter about 2/3 with water. Place the thermometer in water.
    3. Set the stop clock at zero and note its least count. Start stirring the water when temperature of water is at about 70'C note it and start the stop clock and note temperature after every minute.

    OBSERVATIONS

    1. Least count Thermometer = 0.05'C
    2. Room temperature = Tr = 23
    3. Least count of stopwatch = 0.01sec
    4. Starting temperature = 23 + 40 = 63'C

    RESULT:

    1. The value of cooling constant for different time interval are same within the experimental errors. Hence Newton's law of cooling is verified.
    2. The graph between log of excess temperature and time is straight line that verified Newton's law of cooling.

    SOURCES OF ERROR:

    1. Change in temperature of the surrounding.
    2. Blow of air is not constant.
    3. The least count of thermometer being large.
    4. Error in reading thermometer.
    5. Human error.

    PRECAUTIONS:

    1. The water should be stirred gently and continuously for uniform cooling.
    2. Calorimeter must not place on good conductor of heat.
    3. The temperature of surrounding must not change suddently.
    4. Thermometer reading should be observed normally.
